# Varanasi Street Food Guide - Context for Kiro

## Project Overview
This is a local guide application for Varanasi (Banaras) street food culture, helping locals and tourists discover authentic food experiences.

## Cultural Context - Varanasi Food Scene

### Key Local Terms (Banarasi Slang)
- **Kachori** = Fried bread stuffed with lentils
- **Chaat** = Savory snack category with tangy-spicy flavors
- **Tikha** = Spicy/hot
- **Meetha** = Sweet
- **Bharpoor** = Heavy/filling
- **Thela** = Street food cart
- **Dona** = Disposable leaf plate
- **Ghat** = River steps (Ganges)
- **Nimish/Malaiyo** = Winter milk foam dessert
- **Laal** = Red (used for tomato chaat)
- **Chura** = Flattened rice/poha
- **Gulkand** = Rose petal preserve

### Authentic Varanasi Street Foods

1. **Kachori Sabzi**
   - Location: Kachori Gali, Vishwanath Lane
   - Time: Early morning (6-11 AM)
   - Price: ₹30-50
   - Traffic: Low before 8 AM, gets crowded by 9 AM
   - Cultural note: Eaten standing, best with roadside chai
   - Description: Crispy fried kachori served with spicy potato curry

2. **Tamatar Chaat**
   - Location: Dashashwamedh Ghat area
   - Time: Evening (5-10 PM)
   - Price: ₹40-60
   - Traffic: Heavy during Ganga Aarti (6-8 PM)
   - Cultural note: Evening snack after Ganga Aarti, eaten with small wooden spoon
   - Description: Unique tomato-based chaat with spices, a Varanasi specialty

3. **Banarasi Paan**
   - Location: Godowlia, Lanka
   - Time: All day (10 AM - 11 PM)
   - Price: ₹20-200 (varies by ingredients)
   - Traffic: Moderate all day
   - Cultural note: Post-meal tradition, fold and eat in one go. Spit carefully!
   - Description: Betel leaf with gulkand, coconut, and various ingredients

4. **Malaiyo (Nimish)**
   - Location: Nadesar, Sigra
   - Seasonal: December to February only
   - Time: 6-10 AM only
   - Price: ₹40-80
   - Traffic: High on weekends
   - Cultural note: Made with dew-chilled milk, must try before 9 AM!
   - Description: Creamy milk foam dessert, available only in winter mornings

5. **Chooda Matar**
   - Location: Luxa, Bengali Tola
   - Time: Morning (7 AM - 12 PM)
   - Price: ₹25-40
   - Season: Winter specialty (Nov-Feb)
   - Traffic: Low morning
   - Cultural note: Eaten from dona (leaf bowl) with fingers
   - Description: Flattened rice with spiced peas, winter morning favorite

6. **Launglata**
   - Location: Thateri Bazaar
   - Time: All day (9 AM - 8 PM)
   - Price: ₹60-100 per kg
   - Traffic: Moderate
   - Cultural note: Traditional gift item for guests, breaks with satisfying crunch
   - Description: Sweet crispy pastry with clove flavor, perfect tea snack

### Traffic Patterns
- **Morning Rush**: 8-10 AM (office hours, school time)
- **Evening Peak**: 6-8 PM (Ganga Aarti time at ghats)
- **Low Traffic**: Before 8 AM, after 10 PM
- **Weekend Crowds**: 2-3x normal at popular spots
- **Festival Times**: Extremely crowded during Diwali, Dev Deepawali

### Local Eating Customs
- Most street food is eaten standing or at small benches
- Use dona (leaf plates) or newspapers as plates
- Paan should be chewed slowly, not swallowed whole
- Morning foods (like Malaiyo) disappear by noon
- Bargaining is acceptable at thelawalas (cart vendors)
- Always eat with right hand when using fingers
- Remove shoes before sitting at some traditional places

### Price Ranges (2024-2025)
- Budget: ₹20-50 per item
- Mid-range: ₹50-100 per item
- Premium (special paan, sweets): ₹100-200

### Geographic Areas in Varanasi
- **Godowlia**: Central market area, shopping and paan
- **Vishwanath Lane**: Temple area, morning breakfast spots
- **Dashashwamedh Ghat**: Main ghat, evening chaat after Aarti
- **Luxa**: Residential area, authentic local food
- **Bengali Tola**: Mixed neighborhood, traditional snacks
- **Lanka**: University area, student-friendly prices
- **Sigra**: Modern residential area
- **Nadesar**: Traditional residential area
